The Boeing 737, a workhorse of the skies, boasts a legacy of reliability and versatility. With its compact size and efficiency, it's a favorite for short to medium-haul flights. Passengers appreciate its comfort, and airlines rely on its economical performance to connect destinations worldwide.

General Information:

  • Manufacturer: Boeing Commercial Airplanes
  • Model: Boeing 737-800
  • First Flight: July 31, 1997
  • Introduction to Service: April 1998

Dimensions:


  • Length: 129 feet, 6 inches (39.5 meters)
  • Wingspan: 117 feet, 5 inches (35.8 meters)
  • Height: 41 feet, 2 inches (12.6 meters)

Performance:


  • Maximum Takeoff Weight (MTOW): Approximately 174,200 pounds (79,010 kilograms)
  • Maximum Range: Approximately 3,850 nautical miles (7,130 kilometers)
  • Cruise Speed: Approximately Mach 0.785 (511 mph or 823 km/h)

Capacity:


  • Typical Seating Capacity: 162 passengers in a two-class configuration
  • Maximum Seating Capacity: Up to 189 passengers in a high-density configuration

Engines:


  • Two turbofan engines mounted under the wings. Common engine options included the CFM International CFM56-7B series.

Cockpit Crew:


  • Two pilots (flight deck crew)

Cabin Configuration:


  • Typically, the 737-800 features a single-class or two-class configuration, depending on the airline's preference.

Range Variants:


  • The Boeing 737-800 is known for its versatility, suitable for both short-haul and medium-haul flights.

Notable Features:


  • Part of the Boeing 737 Next Generation (NG) family, known for its improved efficiency and performance compared to earlier 737 models.
  • The 737-800 offers a balance of capacity and range, making it a popular choice for various airline routes.
  • Known for its reliability and low operating costs, contributing to its popularity among airlines.

Production:


  • The Boeing 737-800 was produced from the late 1990s until the early 2020s when production shifted to the 737 MAX series.
