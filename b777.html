The Boeing 777, a marvel of aviation, offers a perfect blend of efficiency and comfort. With its extended range, spacious cabin, and advanced technology, it's a favorite among airlines for long-haul flights. The 777 sets the standard for modern air travel, ensuring passengers arrive at their destination in style.

General Information:

  • Manufacturer: Boeing Commercial Airplanes
  • Model: Boeing 777-300ER
  • First Flight: February 24, 2003
  • Introduction to Service: March 10, 2004

Dimensions:


  • Length: 242 feet, 4 inches (73.9 meters)
  • Wingspan: 199 feet, 11 inches (60.9 meters)
  • Height: 61 feet, 5 inches (18.7 meters)

Performance:


  • Maximum Takeoff Weight (MTOW): Approximately 775,000 pounds (351,534 kilograms)
  • Maximum Range: Approximately 7,370 nautical miles (13,650 kilometers)
  • Cruise Speed: Approximately Mach 0.84 (560 mph or 901 km/h)

Capacity:


  • Typical Seating Capacity: Up to 550 passengers in a two-class configuration
  • Maximum Seating Capacity: Up to 550 passengers in a high-density configuration

Engines:


  • Two turbofan engines mounted under the wings. Common engine options included the General Electric GE90-115B or the Pratt & Whitney PW4090.

Cockpit Crew:


  • Two pilots (flight deck crew)

Cabin Configuration:


  • Typically, the 777-300ER features a two-class or three-class configuration, depending on the airline's preference.

Range Variants:


  • The 777-300ER is known for its extended range capabilities, making it suitable for long-haul and ultra-long-haul flights.

Notable Features:


  • The 777-300ER is an extended-range version of the Boeing 777 series, offering increased capacity and range compared to the base 777-200.
  • It features advanced aerodynamics and fuel-efficient engines, making it a popular choice for long-haul flights.
  • The aircraft is equipped with modern amenities and passenger comforts, including larger windows, quieter cabins, and spacious interiors.

Production:


  • The Boeing 777-300ER has been in production since the early 2000s and continues to be a popular choice for airlines worldwide.
