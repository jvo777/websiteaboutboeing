The Boeing 747-400 is a popular wide-body commercial aircraft that has been used by airlines around the world. Here is a specification list for the Boeing 747-400:

General Information:

  • Manufacturer: Boeing Commercial Airplanes
  • Model: Boeing 747-400
  • First Flight: April 29, 1988
  • Introduction to Service: February 9, 1989

Dimensions:


  • Length: 231 feet, 10 inches (70.6 meters)
  • Wingspan: 211 feet, 5 inches (64.3 meters)
  • Height: 63 feet, 8 inches (19.4 meters)

Performance:


  • Maximum Takeoff Weight (MTOW): Approximately 987,000 pounds (447,700 kilograms)
  • Maximum Range: Approximately 7,260 nautical miles (13,420 kilometers)
  • Cruise Speed: Approximately Mach 0.855 (570 mph or 918 km/h)

Capacity:


  • Typical Seating Capacity: 416 passengers in a three-class configuration
  • Maximum Seating Capacity: Up to 524 passengers in a high-density configuration

Engines:


  • Four turbofan engines mounted on pylons under the wings. Common engine options included Pratt & Whitney PW4056, PW4056, General Electric CF6-80C2B1F, and Rolls-Royce RB211-524G/H.

Cockpit Crew:


  • Two pilots (flight deck crew)

Cabin Configuration:


  • Typically, the 747-400 featured a two-deck configuration, with a lower deck used for seating and cargo.

Range Variants:


  • The Boeing 747-400 had several range variants, including the 747-400, 747-400ER (Extended Range), and 747-400ERF (Extended Range Freighter), each with different range and payload capabilities.

Notable Features:


  • The 747-400 introduced a two-pilot glass cockpit, which replaced the traditional three-person flight crew.
  • The aircraft featured advanced aerodynamics and improved fuel efficiency compared to earlier 747 models
  • The ability to fly long-haul routes with a large passenger capacity made it popular with airlines for international travel.

Production:


  • The Boeing 747-400 was produced from the late 1980s to the mid-2000s, with various customer orders and configurations.
